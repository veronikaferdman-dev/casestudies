Case Study — Operationalizing AI Quality Evaluation

Most AI teams ship model output before anyone has decided what “good” actually sounds like, reads like, or means.

This is the story of building the system that answered that question for an enterprise AI assistant — from zero, at scale, with no playbook to follow.

The Problem

A large-scale business AI assistant was scaling rapidly across multiple advertiser surfaces with no shared standard for evaluating the quality, tone, or accuracy of what it said. Individual contributors were making judgment calls in isolation — no calibration process, no grading methodology, no documentation of what failure even looked like, and no way to know if the product was getting better or worse over time.

The risk wasn’t just inconsistency. It was shipping AI outputs that no one had formally decided were good enough — with real potential to give business owners inaccurate guidance and erode trust in the product.

The Approach

Rather than writing guidelines and hoping they’d stick, the goal was an operational system — one that could run repeatedly, surface failure patterns, and improve over time.

  1. 01

    Define the standard

    Establish evaluation criteria before touching a single prompt — what does a passing output look like across tone, accuracy, safety, and UX fit?

  2. 02

    Build the golden query set

    Write 80+ golden-set prompts across 7 product surfaces, covering edge cases, failure-prone scenarios, and high-stakes use cases.

  3. 03

    Run calibrations, not passive reviews

    Facilitate calibration sessions with cross-functional partners — not to rubber-stamp outputs, but to surface disagreement, align on judgment, and document the reasoning.

  4. 04

    Build feedback loops

    Design RLHF-informed grading cycles with structured thumbs-down and FAIL analysis, so every failure became an input to the next iteration.

What Was Built

Working as the sole content design owner across 10+ concurrent workstreams:

  • Infrastructure

    Full model evaluation infrastructure — grading standards, calibration protocol, prompt library, failure taxonomy, and iteration process — none of which existed before.

  • Benchmarks

    80+ golden-set prompts across 7 product surfaces, designed to expose the model’s full failure surface under real-world conditions.

  • Governance

    A Quality Standards Guide covering evaluation methodology, grading rubrics, and decision frameworks — written for one team, adopted by adjacent teams as shared org infrastructure.

  • Training

    A training program for 19 content designers on AI evaluation tooling and methodology, including an AI Building Quick Start Guide for Content Design.

  • Delivery

    180+ total artifacts across evaluation, navigation, notifications, onboarding, and AI-driven surfaces — all delivered concurrently, on deadline.

  • Automation

    A multi-agentic knowledge-maintenance workflow that monitored UI changes and flagged necessary updates automatically — improving the quality and reliability of the assistant while saving design and engineering 20+ hours of manual work weekly.
